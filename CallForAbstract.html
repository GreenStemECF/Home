Green STEM Educational and Charitable Foundation (www.greenstem.org.ng) focuses on promoting Research, Innovation and Commercialisation of Research (RIC) as the sustainable pathway to industrialisation of Nigeria and Africa. We believe that strategic synergy between research institutions, government agencies and companies is required to innovate products and services that would improve national productivity and sustainable development.

The 5th International Green STEM Symposium 2026 (GSS2026) seeks to explore how industrialisation of the Nigerian economy can be facilitated through creative and innovative funding of research and innovation. We therefore invite Lecturers, Researchers, Professionals, Practitioners, Entrepreneurs, Innovators, PhD Students, PostDocs, Government Agencies, and NGOs to submit an abstract for oral or poster presentation.

"Funding is the oxygen of innovation. Without deliberate frameworks to finance research and commercialisation, Nigeria's industrial potential will remain unrealised."

Submission Criteria for the Plenary Session

Abstracts submitted must be a product of scientific research, although any methodology may be adopted — for example: Experimental, Modelling, Mixed Method, Case Study, Qualitative Research, and others. Research may be transdisciplinary, interdisciplinary, or multidisciplinary, but the problem of study must be linked to sustainable industrialisation to create products, services, processes, engineered systems, social systems, business models, government policies, and so forth.

The author(s) will make a presentation during the Symposium. After the Symposium, interested author(s) can submit a full article for publication in DAPresses Journals:

  • Dialogic STEM Journal (ISSN: 3141-2122)
  • Dialogic Society Journal (ISSN: 3141-2130)

Both journals are available at www.dapresses.com.
